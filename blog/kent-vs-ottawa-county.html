Kent County vs. Ottawa County

Which West Michigan county is the better bet for land investors?

Home → Blog → Kent County vs. Ottawa County

March 28, 2026 • 6 min read

If you're investing in West Michigan land, chances are you're looking at either Kent County (Grand Rapids area) or Ottawa County (Holland/Zeeland). Both have strong fundamentals, but they serve different investor profiles. Here's the breakdown.

Kent County — The Established Market

Pros:

  • Largest metro area in West Michigan — deep buyer pool
  • Strong demand for buildable lots near schools and amenities
  • More established real estate infrastructure (realtors, title companies, lenders)
  • Higher exit liquidity — easier to sell

Cons:

  • Higher entry cost — expect to pay $50K+ for usable parcels
  • Fierce competition at auction — often 20%+ above reserve
  • Slower ROI — appreciation tied to broader market trends

Ottawa County — The Growth Play

Pros:

  • Faster population growth (15.1% vs 12.4%)
  • Lower entry point — solid parcels under $40K
  • Lake Michigan proximity drives seasonal rental demand
  • Emerging development — infrastructure improvements opening new areas

Cons:

  • Smaller market — fewer buyers when you exit
  • More zoning complexity — some townships have strict ag preservation
  • Wetland concerns in Holland/Zeeland areas

    • My Take

    For new investors or those with limited capital, Ottawa County makes more sense. The lower entry point and less competition give you room to learn the ropes without risking a massive capital outlay.

    For experienced investors with a longer hold horizon, Kent County is the safer bet. The liquidity and buyer pool make it easier to exit when the time is right.

    Both counties have a place in a diversified land portfolio — consider splitting your capital between the two.

5 Mistakes to Avoid When Buying Raw Land

The costliest errors new land investors make — and how to dodge them

Home → Blog → 5 Mistakes to Avoid

March 15, 2026 • 5 min read

I've made every mistake in this list. Some cost me a few hundred dollars, others cost me thousands. Here's what I wish I'd known before my first land purchase.

1. Skipping the Title Search

Your first instinct might be to grab a "deal" at auction for $5,000. But if there's a surviving mortgage, you're on the hook for it. A $150 title search would have saved me $12,000 on a parcel in Calhoun County.

2. Not Visiting the Property

I once bought a "10‑acre parcel" based on the tax records. Showed up to find 6 acres were a marshy wetland, inaccessible from the road. Always visit before you close.

3. Ignoring Zoning Restrictions

Bought a nice 5‑acre lot in Barry County, planned to build a spec home. Found out after closing it was zoned agricultural — no building permits allowed without a variance (which was denied). Know the zoning before you bid.

4. Overlooking Access Issues

A parcel might look accessible, but without road frontage or an easement, you can't develop it. I always check that the property has at least 33 feet of frontage on a public road — or a recorded easement.

5. Not Factoring in Carrying Costs

Your purchase price is only the start. Property taxes, insurance, and (if financing) loan payments add up. On a $30,000 parcel, carrying costs can easily hit $3,000/year. Budget for 12-24 months of holding costs before you buy.

How to Avoid These Mistakes

  • Always get a title search — $150 well spent
  • Visit every property — Even a drone flyover helps
  • Verify zoning — Call the county planning office
  • Check access — Verify road frontage and easements
  • Budget carrying costs — Plan for 18+ months of hold time

The land investing learning curve is steep, but these mistakes are avoidable. Take your time on due diligence, and you'll save far more than you'll ever lose.

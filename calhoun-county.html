Why Calhoun County?

Calhoun County, anchored by Battle Creek, offers some of the most affordable land prices in lower Michigan. With less investor competition than Kent or Ottawa counties, it's an ideal market for new land investors or those looking to build a portfolio without breaking the bank.

Key Zoning & Land Use

Residential (R1, R2)

  • Best for: Building modest homes, rental properties
  • Typical lot size: 0.25 – 1 acre
  • Permits required: Building permit, well/septic if needed
  • Avg. price range: $20K – $80K per acre

Agricultural (AG)

  • Best for: Row crops, hobby farms, solar farms
  • Typical lot size: 5 – 80 acres
  • Permits required: Minimal for agricultural use
  • Avg. price range: $3K – $7K per acre

Recreational / Forest

  • Best for: Hunting leases, timber investment, recreation
  • Typical lot size: 10 – 100+ acres
  • Permits required: Limited
  • Avg. price range: $1,500 – $4,000 per acre

Where to Find Deals

  1. Calhoun County Tax Auction — Annual sale typically in March/April. Check the County Treasurer's website.
  2. MLS — Limited vacant land inventory, but worth checking with a local realtor.
  3. Direct Mail — Highly effective in less competitive markets — target owners of 5+ acre parcels.
  4. OnX Hunt — Use for parcel boundaries and ownership research.

Due Diligence Checklist

  • ✓ Title search (~$150)
  • ✓ Zoning verification (Calhoun County Planning: 269-969-2560)
  • ✓ Perc test (for any septic) — important in areas with clay soils
  • ✓ Flood zone determination
  • ✓ Utility verification
  • ✓ Survey recommended for parcels >10 acres

Why Investors Are Watching Calhoun County

  • Low entry point — Can acquire 10+ acres for under $40K
  • Less competition — Fewer investors active, more negotiating power
  • Agricultural demand — Strong farming community with potential for lease income
  • Development potential — Battle Creek area seeing gradual growth

Risks to Watch

  • Slower appreciation — Price growth lags urban counties; hold for long term
  • Limited liquidity — Fewer buyers when it comes time to sell
  • Soil quality varies — Some areas have poor drainage; always get a soil map
